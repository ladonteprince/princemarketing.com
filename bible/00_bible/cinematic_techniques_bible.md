# PrinceMarketing.com -- Cinematic & Visual Techniques Bible

## Purpose
This document defines the visual production techniques for all PrinceMarketing marketing materials, product demos, launch videos, and brand content. These are NOT for the user-facing product (which follows the World Bible's UI specs), but for how we visually represent PrinceMarketing in promotional materials.

---

## 1. PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

### Product Screenshots
- Always captured on dark mode (default)
- Browser chrome removed -- show the product, not the browser
- 16:9 for hero images, 1:1 for feature callouts
- Subtle depth-of-field effect: focused element sharp, surrounding UI at 95% opacity
- No fake data -- use realistic demo account data that tells a story

### Customer Photography
- Environmental portraits: business owner in their workplace
- Natural light preferred, supplemented with a single soft key light
- Shallow depth of field (f/2.0-2.8 equivalent)
- Color graded to match brand palette: slightly desaturated, cool shadows, neutral midtones
- Never staged. Never stock. Real people in real environments.

## 2. VIDEO PRODUCTION (Marketing/Promo)

### Shot Vocabulary

| Shot Type | When to Use | Camera Behavior |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Wide establishing** | Opening a sequence, showing context | Slow push-in or static, 24fps |
| **Medium product shot** | Showing a feature in context | Static or subtle track, screen fills 60% of frame |
| **Close-up detail** | Highlighting a specific interaction | Slow zoom, rack focus to element |
| **Over-the-shoulder** | User interacting with product | Handheld stabilized, warm lighting |
| **Screen capture** | Demo walkthrough | Smooth cursor movement, 60fps for fluidity |

### Editing Rhythm
- Cut on action (user taps, screen transitions)
- Average shot length: 3-5 seconds for social, 5-8 seconds for long-form
- No jump cuts. Every cut has visual motivation.
- Transitions: hard cuts for 90% of edits. Subtle cross-dissolve (200ms) for scene changes only.

### Motion Graphics
- Text animations: fade-in + subtle upward drift (8px, 300ms ease-out)
- No text bouncing, spinning, or scaling from zero
- Lower thirds: Inter 600, `#F5F5F5` on `#0A0A0A80` backdrop, 200ms fade in/out
- Number counters: JetBrains Mono, animate from 0 to value over 1.5s ease-out

### Color Grading for Video
- **Shadows:** Cool, pushed toward `#0A0A0A` with slight blue undertone
- **Midtones:** Neutral, slightly desaturated (-10% saturation)
- **Highlights:** Clean white, no warm cast
- **Overall:** High contrast, deep blacks, controlled highlights
- **LUT reference:** Fujifilm ETERNA with +15% contrast

### Sound Design for Video
- **Music:** Minimal electronic. Think: Tycho, Odesza ambient tracks, Bonobo. 90-110 BPM.
- **No voiceover** in product demos -- let the product speak. Text overlays if narration needed.
- **UI sounds in demos:** Subtle, satisfying clicks for interactions. Never cartoonish.
- **Ambient:** Light room tone. No dead silence. No music bed under every second.

## 3. SOCIAL MEDIA VISUAL TEMPLATES

### Instagram (1080x1080 / 1080x1350)
- Background: `#0A0A0A`
- Product screenshot centered with 48px padding
- Feature name in Inter 600, 24px, `#F5F5F5`, bottom-left
- PrinceMarketing wordmark, 12px, `#6B6B6B`, bottom-right

### LinkedIn (1200x627)
- Background: `#0A0A0A`
- Insight text in Inter 600, 32px, `#F5F5F5`, left-aligned with 64px left padding
- Statistic or number in JetBrains Mono, 48px, Royal `#6366F1`
- No product screenshots on LinkedIn unless the post is specifically about a feature

### Twitter/X (1600x900)
- Same principles as LinkedIn
- More whitespace, less text
- Maximum 8 words on image
