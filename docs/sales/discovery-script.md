# Discovery Script — Prevent Objections Before They Land

> The best objection handling is **never letting the objection form**.
> The first 15 minutes of every call are *yours to ask, not theirs to interrogate*.
> Cluely should stay quiet here. This is human work.

---

## STAGE 1 — RAPPORT (3 min)

Goal: prove you did your homework. Earns the right to ask hard questions.

- *"Before we get into anything — I watched your last 3 posts / saw the [specific campaign]. The one about [specific detail] — was that an in-house produce or an agency?"*
- *"How long have you been in this seat?"*
- *"What's the most recent thing you shipped that you're proud of?"*

**Listen for:** what they value (craft? speed? volume? data?). Mirror it back later.

---

## STAGE 2 — CURRENT STATE (5 min)

Goal: get the actual numbers and process. Without this, you're pitching blind.

- *"Walk me through how video happens for you right now — who briefs, who creates, who approves?"*
- *"How many videos a month are you actually shipping?"*
- *"What's that costing you — agency fees, freelancer hours, your team's time?"*
- *"Where does it break — production, approvals, or distribution?"*

**Listen for:** the bottleneck. That's what you'll sell into.

---

## STAGE 3 — DESIRED STATE (4 min)

Goal: get them to articulate the gap *out loud*. People buy what they describe themselves wanting.

- *"If video production wasn't a constraint — what would you ship? What's on the wish list that never gets made?"*
- *"What does 'good' look like 90 days from now? Be specific — number of pieces, channels, results."*
- *"What's the business outcome behind the content? Leads? Sales? Brand awareness with a specific audience?"*
- *"What happens if you don't solve this in the next 90 days?"*

**Listen for:** the cost of inaction. You'll quote it back during pricing.

---

## STAGE 4 — DECISION & BUDGET (3 min)

Goal: qualify hard. Don't pitch unfit prospects — refer them out.

- *"Who else weighs in on this decision?"*
- *"What's your typical process when you sign on a vendor like this?"*
- *"Have you got a budget range in mind, or are we figuring it out together?"*
  - If they dodge: *"Even a ceiling helps — what's the number where you'd have to go to a bigger conversation?"*
- *"What's your timeline — when do you want first content live?"*

**Disqualify (gracefully) if:**
- Budget < $2K/mo and they need volume
- No clear KPI ("just want more engagement")
- Decision sits 3+ levels above and you can't get to them
- Timeline is "someday"

**Refer-out script:**
*"Honest read: at that budget, you're better served by a freelancer than us. Here's a couple of folks I trust — I'll intro you. When you're at [$X/mo] in spend, come back."*

(This earns trust and referrals. Wrong-fit clients destroy your time and reviews.)

---

## STAGE 5 — DIAGNOSIS (mirror back)

Before you pitch — restate what you heard. This is the most important moment in the call.

> *"Let me play that back. You're shipping [X videos/month] today, mostly through [process], and the bottleneck is [thing]. The goal in 90 days is [their specific outcome]. The hesitation isn't capability — it's [risk they named]. Did I get that right?"*

If they say "yes" — they've sold themselves. Pitch is now confirmation, not persuasion.
If they say "not quite" — listen, adjust, ask again. Don't pitch until the mirror lands.

---

## STAGE 6 — TIER MATCH

Now Cluely earns its keep. Based on what they said:

| What you heard | Recommend |
|---|---|
| "We're testing AI for the first time" | **Pilot — $3K/mo, 60 days** |
| "We have budget, want consistency" | **Series — $5K/mo, 8 episodes** |
| "We're scaling content org-wide" | **Brand — $8.5K/mo, 16 episodes** |
| "We need daily presence" | **Always-On — $12.5K/mo** |
| "Multiple regions, multiple campaigns" | **Enterprise — $20K+/mo** |

**Pitch language:**
*"Based on what you described — [restate one specific thing they said] — I'd put you on the [tier]. Here's why: [one outcome-focused reason]. The investment is [price]. What's your reaction?"*

Then **shut up.** First one to speak loses.

## STAGE 8 — CLOSE OR NEXT STEP

Always end with a specific next action and a date.

**If yes:**
*"I'll send the contract by 5pm today. Kickoff call Monday at 10. Sound good?"*

**If maybe:**
*"Let's put a 20-min follow-up on the calendar — what works Tuesday or Wednesday? Bring [missing decision-maker / additional question]."*

**If no:**
*"All good. Mind if I check back in 30 days? In the meantime, anything I can send that'd be useful?"*

**Never end on:** *"Let me know!"* or *"Whenever you're ready!"* — those are losing positions.

---

## POST-CALL RITUAL (5 min, do not skip)

1. Save Cluely transcript to `/clients/[name]/discovery-call.md`
2. Have Cluely auto-generate:
   - Mirror summary (what you heard)
   - Recommended tier + 1-line reason
   - Proposal draft (use cluely-context.md pricing)
   - Follow-up email
3. Send proposal *same day* if call went well. Speed = professionalism.
4. Note in CRM: next-step + date + key phrase they used (use it in the follow-up).
