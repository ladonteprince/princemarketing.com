# Objection Playbook — Glanceable Cheat Sheet

> Keep this open on a side monitor or in Cluely's invisible overlay.
> **Read the bold trigger word, glance the response, then look back at the camera.**
> Every response ends with a question that moves the call forward.

---

## PRICE OBJECTIONS

**"Too expensive"**
> *"Compared to what — a traditional agency at 3x, or doing nothing? Walk me through what you've spent on video in the last 6 months."*

**"Why so much?"**
> *"You're not paying for AI tools — those are $300/mo. You're paying for the system that makes 16 episodes look like one brand, every month, on time. What would it cost you to build that in-house?"*

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> *"I can do less, not cheaper. Want to drop from 8 to 4 episodes a month and stay at quality, or hold volume and stretch payment over 6 months?"*

**"$X is over our budget"**
> *"Got it — what number does work? I'll tell you what fits at that level, honestly, even if it's not us."*

**"Send me your cheapest option"**
> *"Cheapest is the 60-day Pilot at $3K. But before I send anything — what would have to be true at day 60 for you to keep going?"*

---

## TRUST / QUALITY OBJECTIONS

**"AI video looks fake"**
> *"Some does. Mine doesn't — and I'll prove it. Want me to send 3 cuts in your style by Friday before we sign anything?"*

**"Will it match our brand?"**
> *"That's the whole job. I build a custom pipeline per brand — your colors, your character, your voice. First episode is the proof. What's your most 'on-brand' piece you've ever shipped?"*

**"How do I know you can deliver?"**
> *"You don't yet. That's why the Pilot exists — 60 days, 6 episodes, you decide. What's the deal-breaker bar — what would I have to do to fail your test?"*

**"Can you handle our compliance / legal?"**
> *"Yes — usage rights, model releases, music licensing all covered in the SOW. Who on your side reviews creative for legal?"*

---

## COMPETITIVE OBJECTIONS

**"We use Synthesia / Heygen / [tool]"**
> *"Those are tools. We're a production studio that uses tools — including those. The difference is we ship branded series, not template avatars. Want to see the gap on a side-by-side?"*

**"Our agency already does video"**
> *"Great — what's your turnaround per video and your monthly volume? We usually augment agencies on volume, not replace them."*

**"My nephew/intern/freelancer can do AI video"**
> *"Probably can. The question is whether they'll deliver 16 on-brand episodes by the 30th, every month, without you babysitting. When was the last time that happened?"*

**"Why not just hire someone in-house?"**
> *"$80K + benefits + 3 months to ramp + no guarantee they know what they're doing. Or 30 days, half the cost, proven output. Which timeline fits your goals?"*

---

## STALL TACTICS

**"Send me a proposal"**
> *"Happy to. What's the number above which this becomes an automatic no? I want the proposal to land inside your bar, not outside."*

**"I need to think about it"**
> *"Totally fair — what specifically? If it's price, I have options. If it's risk, the Pilot is built for that. Which one?"*

**"I need to talk to my partner / team"**
> *"Who else needs to be in the room? Let's put a 15-min follow-up on the calendar with them — beats forwarding a deck."*

**"Let me get back to you next week"**
> *"Cool — should I save your Pilot slot for [date]? I have one this month and one next. Otherwise it goes to the next person on the call list."*

**"We're not ready yet"**
> *"What needs to be true before you are? I'd rather time it right than push a 'yes' that turns into a hard month."*

---

## SCOPE CREEP / DOWNGRADE TRAPS

**"Can we just do one video to start?"**
> *"We can — $5K project rate. But honest: one video doesn't prove a system. The Pilot at $3K gets you three episodes at lower per-unit cost. Which actually fits your goal?"*

**"Can you throw in [extra deliverable] for free?"**
> *"I can include it if we extend the term — 6 months instead of 3. Otherwise it's an add-on at $X. Either works for me."*

**"Can we start smaller and scale up?"**
> *"That's literally the Pilot. $3K, 60 days, then we step up. When can you start?"*

---

## TIMELINE OBJECTIONS

**"How fast can you start?"**
> *"Contract signed Monday, kickoff call Tuesday, first cut Friday. Standard. What's your launch date pressure?"*

**"We need it next week — too fast?"**
> *"Doable with rush — adds 50%. Want me to scope it that way, or is the launch date flexible?"*

**"We don't need it for 3 months"**
> *"Cool — let's lock the slot now at this month's rate. I'm raising prices in [Q]. What pre-work can we get done before kickoff?"*

---

## DECISION-MAKER GAPS

**"I'm not the decision maker"**
> *"Got it — who is, and how do they decide? Best move: I get them 5 minutes on a follow-up so they're hearing it firsthand, not from your forward."*

**"Our marketing team handles this"**
> *"Perfect — can you intro me to the lead by EOD? I'll send a 90-second Loom showing exactly what we'd ship in their first month so they have something concrete."*

---

## POST-OBJECTION RESETS

When you've handled 2+ objections and the call is dragging:

> *"Pause — what's the actual hesitation? It's not the money or the timeline based on what you've said. What would have to be true for this to be an obvious yes?"*

> *"Fair enough. Want to revisit in 30 days, or is this a soft no? I'd rather know."*

---

## END-OF-CALL ANCHORS

**Trial close:**
> *"If everything I've said is real and the work delivers — does this become a yes?"*

**Real close:**
> *"Want to start with the Pilot? I'll send the contract by 5pm and we kick off Monday."*

**Failed close (don't push):**
> *"All good. I'll follow up in two weeks. Anything I should send between now and then to make the next call shorter?"*
